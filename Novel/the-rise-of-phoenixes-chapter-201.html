Chapter 201: Chapter 201

He sagged against the bedside, relieved. As she stared at in him astonishment, some embarrassment crept over his face. He leaned down and tucked her in once more before hobbling back to his bed. He worked hard at a natural smile as he explained: “I had a nightmare, I thought…”

Feng Zhiwei understood.

All those days and nights as she lingered on the edge of death, he must have stayed by her side just like this. All those long, dreadful nights, he had watched and waited in fear and anxiety. He had dreamed that she no longer breathed, and he had rushed over so many times now that it was already an instinctive reaction, and even now that she was recovering, he would still wake from the nightmare.

How many times had he woken like this and how deep and heavy his concern until he had formed such a compulsive habit?

Feng Zhiwei silently stared up at the ceiling, blinking.

Tears dripped down her face.

…

“Here, medicine.”

“Oh… Yi, Ning Yi, look!”

“Stop playing. Ning Cheng won’t come; Yan Huaishi won’t come; there are no assassins; Hua Qiong is fine… Really, Feng Zhiwei, this trick is already old. Stop messing around and take the medicine.”

“Oh.”

The failed trickster obediently accepted the bowl.

“Let me.” Ning Yi said, ignoring her hand. “Or else you’ll play some other strange trick.”

“Your eyes are not well, how will you feed me.” Feng Zhiwei replied, dodging aside. “I’m afraid you’re going to feed it into my nose.”

“I can see you.” Ning Yi replied simply.

Feng Zhiwei hung her head in silent defeat; she was no unruly child and understood that good medicine tasted bitter, but this medicine was really too terrifying. She bet Child Urine[1] tasted better than this, and even after drinking this medicine everyday, she still could not get used to it. Every time she drank this medicine the more afraid she became.

Some time had passed since she had woken, and other than this horrible medicine, Feng Zhiwei was taken care of better than she had ever been — surrounded by close friends and attended by His Royal Highness. While Feng Zhiwei was too weak to refuse anything, Ning Yi had helped her patiently and delicately, and by the time Feng Zhiwei’s strength grew enough that she could push him away, certain matters had already solidified into habits. To refuse to let him feed her seemed almost ungrateful.

Intimacy was the best method of grinding away hostility and washing away deep rooted resistance. After returning from the edge of death, survivors were more willing to relax and soften their hearts; Ning Yi and Feng Zhiwei had always thought similarly and been able to cooperate, but now after the disasters of this trip, the distance between them fell away and their guard against each other was lowered, leaving in their place a new degree of gentle warmth.

The silver spoon clicked against the porcelain bowl as Ning Yi calmly sat by her bed and fed her. The medicine not only tasted horrifying, it also smelled incredibly pungent, but Ning Yi acted as if nothing could phase him and first brought the silver spoon up to his face to smell its quality before accurately feeding it to her.

Feng Zhiwei stared down at the faintly steaming spoon and then looked up at Ning Yi’s dimmed eyes, so different from his previous brilliant gaze. She felt a weight in her throat and chest, and she obediently opened her mouth and swallowed the medicine.

The courtyard was quiet, and the only sound was the faint whispers of a mouse up above — Young Master Gu was eating his walnuts again. Peace reigned.

After a persistent Ning Yi had fed her the entire bowl of Medicine, Feng Zhiwei let out a long breath; but before she could speak, a snowy handkerchief was brought over to her mouth and gently pressed against her lips.

“Don’t move.”

The last dredges of medicine were wiped away and Feng Zhiwei opened her mouth again, and a sweet fruit was placed on her tongue.

“Longxi’s Pickled Sweet Plum.” Ning Yi mumbled through his own. “I quite like it.”

“I’m being treated like a child.” Feng Zhiwei smiled, “But when I was sick as a little girl, I never had care like this.”

“Then I’ll make up for the past.” Ning Yi chuckled, his hand on her hair.

Feng Zhiwei’s heart quivered and she turned away, looking out at the autumn sun. “Today’s pretty nice.”

“Let’s go sit outside and you can have some fresh air.”

Young Master Gu floated down, a hand lifted the patient and a hand lifting the soft bed, helpfully carrying Feng Zhiwei to spare His Highness the trouble, and the Highness who had just been looking forward to carrying the beauty in his arms could only follow, sad and angry.

Young Master Gu clumsily set up the bed and then buried Feng Zhiwei under three layers of blankets. Feng Zhiwei struggled valiantly under the thick cloth, only her eyes peeking out from them as she struggled to say thanks.

A satisfied Young Master Gu returned to his rooftop to continue eating his walnuts as Feng Zhiwei called out for Ning Yi’s rescue: “Quick… I’m suffocating.”

Ning Yi smiled as he moved away the two outer layers and rearranged the messy bed, somewhat gleeful as he announced: “See, you need me.”

Such a narcissist, but Feng Zhiwei had to admit: “For now.”

“It’s nice even if it’s temporary.” Ning Yi replied, sitting down beside her. “I hate it when you push yourself too much.”

Feng Zhiwei fell silent and they sat together quietly watching the autumn wind. The dappled red maples lined the yard alongside an assortment of chrysanthemums ranging from shades of purple to yellow. It was beautifully lonely as a flock of migrating northern goose arced through the high, clear sky, scattering wisps of cloud with their dark wings.

One lay as the other sat, and they shared the quiet peace of the late autumn day. The listened to the flowers and leaves fall from branches and little birds rustling while dew dripped from lotus leaves. It did not matter what they could or could not see, for the view was in their heart, and they were in each other’s hearts.

The quiet lasted for a long time, and then finally the hurried patter of anxious footsteps neared. Feng Zhiwei looked up and slowly smiled.

“Take care.” She said.

Ning Yi slowly lowered himself, his breath warm against her ear. Feng Zhiwei tried to move aside, but there was nowhere for her to go as his lips neared and neared and finally touched her ear. He whispered quietly, voice as gentle as his lips against her skin: “Wait for me.”

Feng Zhiwei did not reply, and he gently bit down on her earlobe, neither hard nor light. She felt herself prickle and itch, but for some reason the feeling did not come from her ear.

His breath was gorgeous and cool like an autumn cloud gently curling around her. His gaze twinkled like a floating boat, as if here to capture a heart and bring it away.

She could not reply, but he refused to leave without her answer. He breathed gently beside her, his breath calm and shallow and light, afraid that he would startle her weak body. But still, her refused to let go, and Feng Zhiwei could only smile helplessly, pushing him aside and clutching her ear before finally saying: “I have to wait for you so we can return to Dijing together.”

Feng Zhiwei mengerti.

Sepanjang hari dan malam saat dia bertahan di ambang kematian, dia pasti tetap di sisinya seperti ini. Sepanjang malam yang panjang dan mengerikan itu, dia memperhatikan dan menunggu dalam ketakutan dan kecemasan. Dia telah bermimpi bahwa dia tidak lagi bernapas, dan dia telah bergegas berkali-kali sekarang karena itu sudah merupakan reaksi naluriah, dan bahkan sekarang setelah dia pulih, dia masih akan terbangun dari mimpi buruk.

Berapa kali dia terbangun seperti ini dan seberapa dalam dan berat kekhawatirannya sampai dia membentuk kebiasaan kompulsif seperti itu?

Feng Zhiwei diam-diam menatap langit-langit, berkedip.

“Berhenti bermain. Ning Cheng tidak akan datang; Yan Huaishi tidak akan datang; tidak ada pembunuh; Hua Qiong baik-baik saja… Sungguh, Feng Zhiwei, trik ini sudah kuno. Berhenti main-main dan minum obatnya. ”

Oh.

Penipu yang gagal dengan patuh menerima mangkuk itu.

Biarkan aku. Ning Yi berkata, mengabaikan tangannya. “Atau kamu akan memainkan trik aneh lainnya.”

“Matamu tidak sehat, bagaimana kamu akan memberiku makan.” Feng Zhiwei menjawab, menghindar ke samping. “Aku takut kamu akan memasukkannya ke hidungku.”

“Aku bisa melihatmu.” Ning Yi menjawab dengan sederhana.

Feng Zhiwei menundukkan kepalanya dalam kekalahan diam; Dia bukan anak yang sulit diatur dan mengerti bahwa obat yang baik terasa pahit, tetapi obat ini benar-benar terlalu menakutkan. Dia bertaruh Child Urine [1] terasa lebih enak dari ini, dan bahkan setelah meminum obat ini setiap hari, dia masih belum terbiasa. Setiap kali dia meminum obat ini, dia menjadi semakin takut.

Beberapa waktu telah berlalu sejak dia bangun, dan selain obat yang mengerikan ini, Feng Zhiwei dirawat lebih baik daripada sebelumnya – dikelilingi oleh teman-teman dekat dan dihadiri oleh Yang Mulia. Sementara Feng Zhiwei terlalu lemah untuk menolak apapun, Ning Yi telah membantunya dengan sabar dan hati-hati, dan pada saat kekuatan Feng Zhiwei tumbuh cukup besar sehingga dia bisa mendorongnya pergi, beberapa hal telah menjadi kebiasaan. Menolak untuk memberinya makan sepertinya hampir tidak bersyukur.

Keintiman adalah metode terbaik untuk menghilangkan permusuhan dan menghilangkan perlawanan yang mengakar. Setelah kembali dari ambang kematian, para penyintas lebih rela untuk rileks dan melembutkan hati mereka; Ning Yi dan Feng Zhiwei selalu berpikiran sama dan dapat bekerja sama, tetapi sekarang setelah bencana perjalanan ini, jarak antara mereka menjauh dan penjagaan mereka terhadap satu sama lain diturunkan, meninggalkan tingkat kehangatan lembut yang baru di tempat mereka.

Sendok perak berbunyi klik di mangkuk porselen saat Ning Yi dengan tenang duduk di samping tempat tidurnya dan memberinya makan. Obat itu tidak hanya terasa mengerikan, tetapi juga baunya sangat menyengat, tetapi Ning Yi bertindak seolah-olah tidak ada yang bisa menahannya dan pertama-tama membawa sendok perak ke wajahnya untuk mencium kualitasnya sebelum secara akurat memberinya makan.

Feng Zhiwei menatap sendok yang mengepul samar dan kemudian menatap mata Ning Yi yang redup, sangat berbeda dari tatapan brilian sebelumnya. Dia merasakan beban di tenggorokan dan dadanya, dan dia dengan patuh membuka mulutnya dan menelan obatnya.

Halaman itu sunyi, dan satu-satunya suara adalah bisikan samar seekor tikus di atas – Tuan Muda Gu sedang makan kenari lagi. Damai berkuasa.

Setelah Ning Yi yang gigih memberinya makan semangkuk Obat, Feng Zhiwei menghela napas panjang; tetapi sebelum dia bisa berbicara, saputangan salju dibawa ke mulutnya dan dengan lembut ditempelkan di bibirnya.

“Jangan bergerak.”

Keruk obat terakhir dihapus dan Feng Zhiwei membuka mulutnya lagi, dan buah manis diletakkan di lidahnya.

Acar Plum Manis Longxi. Ning Yi bergumam sendiri. Aku sangat menyukainya.

Aku diperlakukan seperti anak kecil. Feng Zhiwei tersenyum, “But ketika Aku sakit sebagai seorang gadis kecil, Aku tidak pernah memiliki perawatan seperti ini. ”

“Kalau begitu aku akan menebus masa lalu.” Ning Yi terkekeh, tangannya di rambutnya.

Hati Feng Zhiwei bergetar dan dia berbalik, memandang matahari musim gugur. Hari ini cukup bagus.

Mari kita duduk di luar dan Kamu bisa menghirup udara segar.

Tuan Muda Gu melayang ke bawah, sebuah tangan mengangkat pasien dan sebuah tangan mengangkat tempat tidur empuk, membantu membawa Feng Zhiwei untuk menyelamatkan Yang Mulia dari masalah, dan Yang Mulia yang baru saja berharap untuk membawa keindahan di lengannya hanya bisa mengikuti, sedih dan marah.

Tuan Muda Gu dengan canggung mengatur tempat tidur dan kemudian mengubur Feng Zhiwei di bawah tiga lapis selimut. Feng Zhiwei berjuang dengan gagah berani di bawah kain tebal, hanya matanya yang mengintip dari mereka saat dia berjuang untuk mengucapkan terima kasih.

Tuan Muda Gu yang puas kembali ke atap rumahnya untuk melanjutkan makan kenari saat Feng Zhiwei berseru untuk penyelamatan Ning Yi: “Cepat … aku tercekik.”

Ning Yi tersenyum saat dia memindahkan dua lapisan luar dan menata ulang tempat tidur yang berantakan, agak gembira saat dia mengumumkan: “Lihat, kamu membutuhkan Aku.”

Benar-benar seorang narsisis, tetapi Feng Zhiwei harus mengakui: “Untuk saat ini.”

“Menyenangkan meski hanya sementara.” Ning Yi menjawab, duduk di sampingnya. “Aku benci kalau kamu terlalu memaksakan diri.”

Feng Zhiwei terdiam dan mereka duduk bersama sambil mengamati angin musim gugur. Maple merah belang-belang berjejer di halaman di samping berbagai macam krisan mulai dari warna ungu hingga kuning. Sangat sepi saat kawanan angsa utara yang bermigrasi muncul di langit yang tinggi dan cerah, menyebarkan gumpalan awan dengan sayap gelap mereka.

Yang satu berbaring sementara yang lain duduk, dan mereka berbagi kedamaian yang tenang di akhir musim gugur. Bunga dan daun berguguran dari dahan dan burung-burung kecil bergemerisik sementara embun menetes dari daun teratai. Tidak peduli apa yang mereka bisa atau tidak bisa lihat, karena pemandangan itu ada di hati mereka, dan mereka ada di hati masing-masing.

Keheningan berlangsung lama, dan akhirnya derap langkah kaki yang terburu-buru mendekat. Feng Zhiwei mendongak dan perlahan tersenyum.

“Hati hati.” Dia berkata.

Ning Yi perlahan menurunkan tubuhnya, napasnya hangat di telinganya. Feng Zhiwei mencoba untuk minggir, tetapi tidak ada tempat baginya untuk pergi saat bibirnya mendekat dan mendekat dan akhirnya menyentuh telinganya. Dia berbisik pelan, suaranya selembut bibirnya menyentuh kulitnya: “Tunggu aku.”

Feng Zhiwei tidak menjawab, dan dia dengan lembut menggigit daun telinganya, baik keras maupun ringan. Dia merasa dirinya berduri dan gatal, tetapi untuk beberapa alasan perasaan itu tidak keluar dari telinganya.

Nafasnya indah dan sejuk seperti awan musim gugur yang dengan lembut melingkari di sekelilingnya. Tatapannya berkelap-kelip seperti perahu apung, seolah di sini untuk menangkap hati dan membawanya pergi.

Dia tidak bisa menjawab, tetapi dia menolak untuk pergi tanpa jawabannya. Dia bernapas lembut di sampingnya, napasnya tenang dan dangkal dan ringan, takut dia akan mengejutkan tubuh lemahnya. Tapi tetap saja, dia menolak untuk melepaskannya, dan Feng Zhiwei hanya bisa tersenyum tak berdaya, mendorongnya ke samping dan memegangi telinganya sebelum akhirnya berkata: “Aku harus menunggumu agar kita bisa kembali ke Dijing bersama.”
